How We Began

A kindred born out of distance from home

As more sons and daughters of Anambra State arrived in Qatar to work, study, and build new lives, a simple truth became clear: community is what makes a foreign land feel survivable. What began as informal gatherings among a handful of Anambra families in Doha grew, over time, into a structured, registered cultural and welfare organisation — Ogbako Ndi Anambra na Qatar.

Today, ONA represents indigenes from all 21 Local Government Areas of Anambra State, spanning Anambra North, Anambra Central, and Anambra South. We are bound not by where in the State we come from, but by a shared identity, a shared language, and a shared resolve to never let our brothers and sisters stand alone in a foreign land.

Mission & Vision

What guides every decision we make

Our Mission

To be a forum where all Anambrarians living in Qatar meet to know and interact with one another in unity and togetherness; to serve as a place of advising / counselling of each other on how to live in peace and be law-abiding here in the country (Qatar) and back home.

Our Vision

To be the most trusted and well-organised State association in Qatar's Nigerian community — a model of unity, transparency, and brotherhood that other unions look to, and a reliable bridge between our members and home.

Our Guiding Principles

The values behind "Ife Adika Nwanne"

Our motto is not a slogan — it is a practice. Here is how we live it out.

♠

Brotherhood Above Self

Personal differences fade when a brother or sister needs support — in joy, in grief, or in hardship.

♠

Respect for Elders & Tradition

We honour the wisdom of our elders and the customs passed down from Anambra's ancestors.

♠

Inclusion of Every LGA

No Local Government Area is more or less important — every indigene has equal standing in ONA.

♠

Integrity in Leadership

Officers serve, account for funds transparently, and lead by example, not by title.

♠

Discipline & Decorum

As guests in the State of Qatar, our conduct must always reflect honour on Anambra State and Nigeria.

♠

Continuity

We raise our children to know their roots, even while they grow up far from Anambra soil.

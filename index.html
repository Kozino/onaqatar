What We Stand For

A union built on four pillars

Every gathering, every contribution, every conversation at ONA traces back to these commitments.

Brotherhood & Welfare

We look out for one another — visiting the sick, supporting the bereaved, and standing with members through life's seasons, exactly as our motto demands.

Cultural Preservation

From Igbo language to age-grade traditions, we keep the customs of home alive in our children, even thousands of kilometres from Anambra soil.

Unity Across LGAs

Whether from Awka, Onitsha, Nnewi, or Aguata — every local government area of Anambra has a seat and a voice within ONA.

Good Governance

A democratically elected leadership and transparent administration guide the union, accountable to every registered member.

Charitable Outreach

Beyond our community, ONA extends a hand to humanitarian causes — both here in Qatar and back home in Anambra State.

Bridge to Home

We maintain close ties with Anambra State Government and community leadership back home, representing our people abroad with honour.

Who We Are

From 21 local government areas, one family in Doha.

Ogbako Ndi Anambra na Qatar was formed by indigenes of Anambra State who recognised that, far from home, kinship matters more than ever. Today ONA is a registered cultural and welfare association bringing together professionals, traders, and families from every corner of Anambra State.

We gather for cultural festivals, mourn and celebrate together, mentor our youth, and carry the name of Anambra with pride across Qatar.
